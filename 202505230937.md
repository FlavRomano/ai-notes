- Consider a square matrix $A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$
- Let $B\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ have the property that $$A\cdot B= I_n = B\cdot A$$
- $B$ is called **inverse of $A$**, denoted by $A^{-1}$
- If $A^{-1}$ exists 
	- $A$ is **regular/inverible/nonsingular**
	- $A^{-1}$ is **unique**
