The rank-nullity theorem

Given 
- two vector spaces $V$, $W$
- a linear mapping $\Phi: V \to W$

it holds that $$\dim(ker(\Phi)) + \dim(Im(\Phi)) = \dim(V)$$
- this is the fundamental theorem of linear mappings
- it has a lot of consequences.
